About AlexStocks

Real-time market news analytics and sentiment tracking

What is AlexStocks?

AlexStocks helps you understand what people are talking about in the stock market. We track conversations across multiple financial discussion communities and show you the trending stocks, what sentiment surrounds them, and how the buzz is changing over time.

What You'll Find Here

  • Market sentiment pulse: A 24-hour leaning bar that compares positive vs. negative mentions after removing neutral posts.
  • Hourly mentions chart: Interactive chart on the home page to track ticker-linked articles by hour; you can add or clear tickers to focus on your own watchlist.
  • Surge highlights: Daily cards that surface the biggest overall, positive, and negative discussion surges with recent price context.
  • Ticker deep-dives: Dedicated pages that combine price quotes with sentiment timelines, recent mention velocity, and filters for sentiment, source, and date.
  • Live stock prices: Current market prices updated throughout the day and shown alongside sentiment metrics.
  • Discussion volume & users: Quick stats for how many articles and unique users are talking about a ticker today, plus change vs. yesterday.

How It Works

We continuously monitor financial discussion forums and social communities where investors share their thoughts and opinions. The platform automatically identifies which stocks are being mentioned, analyzes whether the sentiment is positive or negative, and presents this information in an easy-to-understand format.

Our goal is to help you see what the investing community is talking about right now, spot emerging trends, and get a sense of market sentimentâ€”all in one place.

Our Data Sources

We gather discussions from multiple major financial communities, including popular forums focused on retail trading, general market discussions, and long-term investing strategies. Stock price information comes from reliable market data providers to give you accurate, real-time pricing alongside the sentiment data.

How to read the pages

  • Home: Shows the current market leaning, the last Reddit scrape time, hourly mention trends, and today's surge cards to spot movers quickly.
  • Ticker pages: Start with a quick stat row (price, today's articles, users, and totals), then use the combined price & sentiment chart to toggle datasets or switch periods (24h, 7d, 30d). Filters below let you narrow by sentiment, source, and date range.
